Research

This research provides a comprehensive analysis of how Generation Z consumes news in the current digital age. It highlights that Gen Z primarily relies on social media and online sources that cater to their interests. They have a preference for highly visual, shareable content that is relatable to their daily life experiences. As opposed to traditional news, Gen Z is more interested in progress and enjoyment, focusing on lifestyle and culture.

To effectively reach this younger generation, media organizations need to provide quick, easy-to-digest information that avoids bias or information overload. Gen Z's short attention span means that they are less likely to dedicate time to reading newspapers and more likely to "snack" on news that interests them. TikTok is becoming a major source of information and education for Generation Z, similar to how Google is for other age groups.

The research also highlights how this trend is affecting industries such as journalism and advertising, as they adapt to the changing ways in which younger generations consume content. Media organizations must tailor their content to Gen Z's preferences by using eye-catching visuals and multimedia, presenting information in a concise and engaging manner, and focusing on issues that affect Gen Z's daily life. News should be delivered via online platforms and social media that Gen Z frequents. Some suggestions for making news relatable and shareable for Gen Z include telling personal stories, using an informal writing style, and incorporating internet culture and memes when appropriate.

However, it is important for media organizations to maintain journalistic integrity by reporting facts and avoiding bias even when catering to Gen Z's interests. Overall, understanding Gen Z's unique perspectives and needs is key to keeping them informed and interested in the news.

In addition to discussing how Gen Z consumes news, the document also provides information regarding how Gen Z workers in the workplace feel about their work. The focus group revealed that they are desensitized by the news, and that news can affect how they feel about work. They prefer designed articles and always want to consume content, but do not want their time to be wasted.
